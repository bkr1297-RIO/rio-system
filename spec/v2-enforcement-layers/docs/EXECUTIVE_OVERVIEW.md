# RIO — EXECUTIVE OVERVIEW (ONE PAGE)

## What This Is

RIO is a control system for AI and automated actions that ensures nothing happens unless it is explicitly approved, bounded, and provable.

It sits between:
- Intelligence (AI, agents, systems)
- Execution (APIs, software, physical systems, biology)

Its job is simple:
turn ideas into actions safely, step-by-step, with full control.

---

## The Core Principle

At every step, the system enforces two rules:

1. Verify what just happened (truth)
2. Explicitly authorize what happens next (permission)

No action can skip either step.

---

## Why This Matters

Most systems fail because:
- Actions automatically trigger more actions
- Permissions silently carry forward
- Systems accumulate power over time

RIO prevents that by:

- Never letting permission flow implicitly
- Requiring a new authorization at every step
- Keeping all actions bounded and auditable

---

## How It Works (Simple Flow)

1. An agent proposes an action  
2. The system evaluates it  
3. A human (or policy) approves it  
4. The system issues a limited authorization (DTT)  
5. The action executes  
6. A receipt is generated as proof  
7. The system repeats the process for the next step  

Every step is controlled. Nothing chains automatically.

---

## The Key Safeguards

- No inherent authority  
  Nothing can act unless explicitly authorized

- Bounded execution  
  Every action has limits (time, scope, effect)

- No accumulation of power  
  Permissions do not carry forward

- Fail closed  
  No proof = no action

- Multi-agent control  
  Multiple systems cannot combine to escape limits

- Cross-system isolation  
  Outputs from one workflow cannot affect another without approval

---

## What Makes It Different

Traditional systems:
- Trust internal behavior
- Monitor after the fact
- Allow implicit chaining

RIO:
- Controls actions before they happen
- Uses explicit authorization at every step
- Works even when the system is complex, opaque, or unpredictable
