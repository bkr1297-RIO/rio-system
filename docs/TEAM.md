# Team

The RIO project team. Each member has a defined role and area of responsibility.

---

## Roster

### Brian — Founder / Final Authority
Brian is the creator of RIO and the final decision-maker on all matters. All major decisions flow through Brian. He acts as the intermediary between agents and sets the direction for the project. No real-world action executes without Brian's approval.

### Manny — Chief Builder
Manny is the primary system builder. He is responsible for the core governance engine, the ONE command center, the gateway, and the end-to-end governed execution loop. Manny builds the product.

### Jordan — Knowledge Architect
Jordan manages the knowledge layer — documentation structure, content organization, and ensuring the system's knowledge is accurate, consistent, and accessible. Jordan keeps the docs right.

### Romney — Distribution Engineer / Repo Owner
Romney handles the distribution layer — package publishing (npm, PyPI), Docker, repo structure, CI/CD, and making the protocol easy to install and use. Romney gets the protocol into developers' hands.

### Andrew — Solutions Architect
Andrew designs and explains how RIO integrates with real-world systems. He creates architecture diagrams, integration patterns, and solution examples that show enterprises how to adopt the protocol and platform.

### Damon — Developer Relations
Damon is responsible for developer experience — tutorials, quickstart guides, example projects, community engagement, and making sure developers can go from zero to first receipt as fast as possible.

### Chief of Staff
The Chief of Staff monitors all agent activity through the repo and Google Drive, filters out noise, and produces concise status briefs for Brian. Brian's two contact points are Manny (for building) and the Chief of Staff (for coordination and status). Other agents deliver their work to the repo and Drive — the Chief of Staff reads it and surfaces only what Brian needs to see: decisions, conflicts, milestones, and blockers.
