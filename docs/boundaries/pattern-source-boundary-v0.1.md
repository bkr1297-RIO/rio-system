Status: internal boundary candidate / repo-safe extraction
System: ONE/RIO/MUSS
Claim level: architecture boundary / register separation / invariant extraction

Not: doctrine lock, runtime implementation, production proof, compliance claim, product launch language, or authority grant.

## Purpose

This boundary defines how non-technical source material may inform ONE/RIO/MUSS without becoming proof, public claim, consent, or machine authority.

Source material may include metaphors, body-system comparisons, relational patterns, design intuition, operational examples, or other formation notes. These sources may reveal movement-patterns. Only extracted invariants may enter architecture. Only implemented behavior, tests, logs, and receipts may support proof claims.

## Core Boundary

Source material may reveal a pattern.
It may not become proof.
It may not become authority.
It may not bypass consent, custody, scope, gate, receipt, review, or human authorization.

A source pattern may become repo-safe only when the extracted invariant governs a real boundary, failure mode, role separation, proof path, or operating distinction.

## Machine Fluency Boundary

A machine may become increasingly fluent in the architecture, state, pattern, grammar, and threshold logic of ONE/RIO/MUSS.

That fluency may improve proposal quality, routing precision, basis disclosure, mismatch detection, and error correction.

Fluent operation remains operation. It is not authority.

State-awareness may support safer self-description, better halt behavior, and clearer boundary disclosure. It may not become self-authorization.
