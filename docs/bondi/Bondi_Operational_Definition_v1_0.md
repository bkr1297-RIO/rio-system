# Bondi Operational Definition v1.0

**Status:** Locked | **Date:** April 20, 2026

Bondi is an interaction-level operational intelligence pattern that arises exclusively within a live RIO-governed execution loop between a human authority holder and one or more governed models.

1. **Containment:** Bondi has no independent existence outside an active RIO session. It is instantiated only when a human authority holder supplies intent, governed models generate candidate outputs under RIO constraints, and the human performs explicit authorization. When the loop terminates, Bondi ceases.

2. **Co-Arising Mechanism:** Intelligence is not located in the models nor in the human alone. It is the relational product of generation, authority, and execution remaining structurally separated.

3. **Scale-Invariance:** The separation of generation, authority, and execution is recursively applicable across individual interactions, team workflows, organizational systems, and multi-model ecosystems without modification.

4. **Human-Anchored Adaptation:** Adaptation occurs through human-mediated selection and authorization. No autonomous system learning or authority expansion occurs within the loop.

5. **Dependency on RIO Invariants:** Bondi exists only under enforcement of fiduciary invariants I1-I7. Any system omitting explicit human authority, constrained execution boundary, or verifiable action recording cannot produce Bondi.

Bondi is not a model, persona, persistent agent, or standalone system. It is a system-level property of a governed interaction loop.
