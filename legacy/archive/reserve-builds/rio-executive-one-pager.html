RIO

AI that can’t act without your approval — and proves it.

RIO is the governance layer that turns AI from a black box into a system of record. Every important action moves through a visible pipeline: intent is evaluated against policy, escalated for approval when needed, executed within bounds, and recorded as a receipt in a tamper-evident ledger.

Pipeline
Intent Policy Approval Execution Receipt Ledger

RIO answers the questions leaders care about: what happened, why it was allowed, who approved it, and where the proof lives.

How It Works

5-step governed loop
1

Intent is proposed

A human or agent requests an action, such as sending a message, updating a record, or triggering a workflow.

2

Policy evaluates risk

RIO checks the action against governance rules, delegation boundaries, and any invariants that must hold.

3

Approval is gathered

If the action crosses a threshold, a human must approve it explicitly before anything happens.

4

Execution runs

The requested action executes only after governance checks succeed and the approval requirements are satisfied.

5

Receipt enters the ledger

A receipt captures what happened and links into the ledger so the history can be replayed and independently verified later.

Executive Explanation

Most AI products focus on speed and convenience. RIO focuses on control without losing usefulness. It allows organizations and individuals to benefit from AI while keeping a hard boundary around what the system may do on its own.

That changes the trust model. Instead of asking people to believe the AI behaved correctly, RIO produces receipts and ledger evidence that can be checked later by security teams, compliance teams, partners, or the user themselves.

Why it matters

  • Important actions cannot quietly happen in a user’s name.
  • Delegation can be granted safely, because boundaries are explicit and enforceable.
  • The system moves from “trust me” to “verify me.”
Stat 1
Model-agnostic

Works across models and agents because governance sits below the model layer.

Stat 2
Zero trust execution

No important action is assumed safe just because an agent suggested it.

Stat 3
Tamper-proof ledger
